The perfect brewing method depends on the type of tea. Generally, black tea requires boiling water, while green tea prefers slightly cooler temperatures. White tea and herbal teas need gentle, cool water. Experiment with temperatures and steeping times to find your perfect cup.

Tea is renowned for its antioxidant properties, which may help lower cholesterol and reduce the risk of heart disease. Green tea has been linked to improved brain function, weight loss, and anti-inflammatory effects. Black tea has been shown to boost metabolism and improve digestion.

The term herbal tea refers to drinks not made from Camellia sinensis. They are the infusions of fruit, leaves, or other plant parts, such as steeps of rosehip, chamomile, or rooibos. These may be called tisanes or herbal infusions to prevent confusion with tea made from the tea plant.
